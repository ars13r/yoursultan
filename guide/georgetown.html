Georgetown, Washington, D.C.

Life in Georgetown

Georgetown offers a unique blend of historic charm and modern convenience, making it one of the most desirable neighborhoods in Washington, D.C. Known for its cobblestone streets, 18th-century architecture, and picturesque waterfront, it’s a neighborhood with character, elegance, and a vibrant community. Living here means easy access to boutique shopping, trendy restaurants, cozy cafes, and high-end stores along M Street and Wisconsin Avenue. It's a lively area, perfect for those who enjoy a walkable, scenic, and sociable environment while still having moments of quiet charm by the Potomac River.

Reasons to live here

  • Georgetown Waterfront Park - A perfect spot for outdoor activities, picnics, and social gatherings with scenic views of the Potomac.
  • Shopping - High-end boutiques, local art galleries, and iconic stores such as Georgetown Park and Cady’s Alley.
  • Historic Tours - Walking tours offer insights into Georgetown's architecture and storied past, including visits to iconic homes like Tudor Place.
  • Georgetown University - A key part of the neighborhood’s identity, the university brings a youthful energy, with student events and cultural activities open to the public.

Events of the Year

  • Georgetown French Market - Held in the spring, this event transforms Book Hill into a Parisian-style open-air market with deals, pastries, and live entertainment.
  • Taste of Georgetown - A fall food festival where visitors can sample the best of Georgetown’s culinary scene.
  • Georgetown GLOW - A winter light art exhibition that features stunning illuminated art installations throughout the neighborhood.
  • Outdoor Movie Nights at Georgetown Waterfront Park - Summer screenings by the waterfront bring the community together for classic films under the stars.
  • Lighting of the Christmas Tree - A holiday tradition where residents gather to kick off the festive season with music and lights.

Where to Eat and Drink

  • Filomena Ristorante - Classic Italian with famous pasta dishes and cozy ambiance.
  • Chez Billy Sud - A charming French bistro with a great wine list and outdoor patio.
  • Fiola Mare - Known for upscale seafood and stunning views of the waterfront.
  • Martin’s Tavern - A historic bar where politicians and presidents, including JFK, once dined.
  • The Sovereign - A Belgian-inspired gastropub with a vast selection of rare beers and a creative menu.

Where to Live

  • Waterfront - A lively, modern area with scenic views of the Potomac River, upscale condos, and bustling restaurants. It’s popular for its chic, urban energy and easy access to outdoor activities like kayaking and walking along the river.
  • Off M Street NW - The heart of Georgetown's commercial district, blending historic charm with vibrant shopping and dining. It has a bustling, energetic vibe with a mix of boutiques, cafes, and nightlife.
  • Dumbarton Oaks - Steeped in history and elegance, this area is anchored by the stunning Dumbarton Oaks gardens and museum. It exudes a refined, scholarly vibe with a strong connection to art, nature, and cultural heritage.
  • Foxhall Village - A quiet, picturesque enclave with charming Tudor-style homes and tree-lined streets. It feels like a peaceful suburban retreat while still being close to the city's action.
  • Berkely - An upscale, residential area known for its spacious homes and serene, green surroundings. It’s family-friendly and feels more like a tranquil escape from the busier parts of Georgetown.
